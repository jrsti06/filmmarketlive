Festival Goer Finds Secret Photos of 'Mooch' Director

NEW YORK, NY — In a chaotic turn of events at the Rejecta Film Festival, a stray flash drive was discovered in the free tote bag handed out to attendees — and what it contained has the indie world buzzing.

The drive, which also included PDFs of headshots and several recipes for “Depression Chili,” reportedly held a folder labeled “DO NOT OPEN.”

“Obviously, I opened it,” said the anonymous attendee. “I thought it was going to be a trailer or something. But no. Just hundreds of selfies of Jeff Ryan staring into mirrors. Different hats. No explanation.”

The filmmaker has yet to comment publicly, though he was seen nervously removing USB drives from other tote bags and muttering something about “artistic vulnerability.”

The incident has sparked debate about privacy, narcissism, and whether tote bags should come with disclaimers.
