Director Says Film Was Inspired by a Dream, Then Admits He Was Just Tired

LOS ANGELES, CA — At a post-screening Q&A hosted in the back room of a vape shop/art gallery, Mooch director Jeff Ryan initially claimed the concept for his film came to him in a vivid, cinematic dream.

"It was like… color, light, sound… but metaphorical, you know?" said Ryan, sipping a yerba mate and looking deeply into the middle distance.

However, within minutes — and after two follow-up questions — Ryan pivoted.

“Okay fine,” he confessed. “I wasn’t dreaming. I had just pulled an all-nighter editing a TikTok about soup and nodded off on the spacebar. Next thing I knew I had a script.”

Eyewitnesses say the crowd didn’t mind the lack of mysticism.

“I respect the honesty,” one attendee said. “Most directors lie to make their movie seem deeper. Jeff just seemed confused and dehydrated.”

As of press time, Ryan was reportedly seen Googling “how to lucid dream on purpose” while whispering “Christopher Nolan would never.”
